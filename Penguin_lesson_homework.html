Hypothesis

Alternative hypothesis or H1 is that penguin body mass increases as culmen length increases.And that this is different between species. The null hypothesis or H0 is that there is no relationship between penguin body mass and penguin culmen length, and that there is no difference between species.

Statistical Methods

Here I create a model exploring the relationship between body mass, culmen length and species. I then check the assumptions of an ANCOVA analysis by doing a qqplot and plotting a residuals vs fitted plot. The qq plot tests for a normal distribution, which the data does have. The fitted vs residual plot tests for linearity.

Summary gives us estimates for the intercepts and slopes. As well, it gives us the p-values for these relationships so you can see if theyre significant. The function anova() completes an anova analysis on the model.

Conclusion

A significant difference was found in the relationship between body mass and culmen length of different species when conducting the ANOVA test on the model as the p-value was equal to < 2e-16. There is also a statistically significant interaction between body mass and species as the p-value was also < 2e-16. This implies that the different penguin species are different sizes as they must have a different average body mass across species. The final ANOVA value implies that there is a significant interaction effect (p = 0.0154) between body mass and species culmen length which suggests that the relationship between culmen length and body mass varies among different species. The values for the summary() function also tell us about the relationship between body mass and culmen length in the different species. For example when comparing Adelie and Chinstrap (speciesChinstrap:culmen_length_mm) the relationship of the Chinstrap penguins is weaker as the value is -35.38, it is also statistically significant as the p-value is 0.047. The difference in the relationship of body mass and culmen length is positive, which would suggest a stronger relationship compared to Adelie. However, the p-value tells us that this is not statisitically significant as the p-value is 0.344.
